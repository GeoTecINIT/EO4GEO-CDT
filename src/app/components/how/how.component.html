How to get started

You can create any educational offer item (study program, module, course, lecture) depending on your needs and taking into account the items hierarchy.

The general structure is Study program - Module - Course - Lecture, whereby each parents aggregates (contains several) children. So, a Study program is a grouping of 1 or more Modules. A Module is a grouping of 1 or more Courses. A Course is a grouping of 1 or more lectures.

Such a grouping could correspond with a semester (academic curriculum), or it could correspond with a topical grouping (for example, a VET training with 3 related courses on SDI's). The structure also allows you to skip any of the items. So, if you don't want modules in a Study program, that's fine, just skip them and specify Courses directly.

Where:

  • A Module is a collection of courses grouped because courses are runned over the same year or semester, or tackle the same topic.
  • A Course is a unit of teaching, a set of lectures or a plan of study on a particular subject, usually leading to an exam or qualification.
  • A Lecture is a formal talk on a serious subject intended to display information or teach people about a particular subject (also known as lessons or classes)


Strategies for creating educational offers

Depending on what you want to model, you will choose a different unit. For example, for an academic curriculum you'll choose a Study program. For a VET training, you can choose a Course (if it's a single course), or even a Lecture (if it's a single class), or a Module if it's a grouping of different courses.

The straightforward way of creating an educational offer is following its hierarchy. So, creating the high level item first, then click on the desired level to add lower level items. Alternatively, the flexibility and modularity of the tool allows to create single items for being grouped later on. That means that contents previously created could be searched and added to a certain educational offer. So, you can also create contents starting from lower levels. For instance creating first a set of single courses, for being later grouped into Modules.
